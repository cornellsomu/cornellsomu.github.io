Introduction

What is Somu Energy?

Problem

1.3 billion people live without electricity, 100 million households in South Asia still use kerosene as a lighting source, and 700 million people will still live off-grid in 2030.

Mission

Our five-year mission is to provide sustainable, clean electricity to two million villagers, create 30,000 jobs, eliminate the use of kerosene for lighting, and improve peopleâ€™s energy access. To scale globally, we use microfranchising, mobile payment systems, and local resources such as schools for distribution.

Solution

Somu Energy empowers entrepreneurs to run a solar energy service and rent battery banks and LED lights to rural villagers. Learn more about the details here!

About

A little bit more about us

  • Motivations

    "Our main motivation for joining the Somu Competition was a desire to help the people of Nepal and work towards a more sustainable future. Somu Energy works towards a number of important issues: the implementation of their solar panels will not only offer rural entrepreneurs an opportunity, the electricity will improve the overall quality of life for the villagers, as well as lower the use of kerosene for more sustainable solar energy. As members of the Cornell International Business Association, we have a strong interest in global businesses and social entrepreneurship, and believe in causes such as Somu Energy. This competition also allows our team to apply business skills to a good cause and make an impact on the world."

  • Importance

    Energy poverty is extremely important to development. Many people around the world are living in extreme poverty, and unable bring themselves out of poverty because of the lack of methods and infrastructure to do so. One of the main reasons why reasons why rural populations are stuck in the cycle of poverty is due to the lack of energy sources, a requirement to sustain economic development, and thus addressing energy poverty will provide opportunities for people living in extreme poverty to empower themselves. The solar panels brought to rural villages in Nepal are especially a great endeavour because not only do these panels provide villagers with energy, but the energy is sustainable and protects the environment. This is why social enterprise is so important. Social enterprise works with the purpose of improving the lives of people in the short term, but also works to create a sustainable world for future generations.

  • Strategy

    Our team is currently utilizing a variety of strategies that we hope will raise significant amounts of money for Somu Energy. For example, we hope to garner support from a number of corporate sponsors who we will feature on our Facebook Page, promote during fundraising events, and perhaps feature in a case competition. At the end of the campaign we will hold a reception for our sponsors to thank them for all of their help and discuss how their generosity will help make a better world.

  • Success

    As Cornell students, we have the resources of a world-class research University at our disposal in addition to the town of Ithaca, which has a history of supporting solar energy use. Our campus alone provides a host of passionate professors and students looking to combat the negative impacts of unsustainable energy use and worldwide poverty. With access to local businesses in addition to larger corporations, our team is ready to raise the necessary funds and take advantage of Cornellâ€™s resources.

Tompkins Solar

Non-profit

Solar Tompkins is a nonprofit community-based organization focused on promoting the rapid adoption of renewable energy in Tompkins County, NY. The Solar Tompkins Board includes volunteer representatives from all 10 towns in Tompkins County, a representative from the Tompkins County Planning Department, Cornell Cooperative Extension (CCE), the Solarize Tompkins SE Program, and the 2015 HeatSmart Tompkins Program Director﻿﻿. Like our 2014 program, we are funded by grants from the Park Foundation again in 2015.

HeatSmart Tompkins advocates all practical means of eliminating fossil fuel use in our homes. To this end, we thank you, Tompkins County community members choosing our HeatSmart campaign or past Switch to Solar campaigns, for joining us in this effort to reduce our carbon footprints and live fossil fuel-free.
